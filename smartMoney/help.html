Calculator Description

Understanding how much more you pay when using a credit card for a purchase can be difficult. The cost of interest calculator will show you how much you'll pay in total based on your purchase amount, your interest rate (APR), your monthly payment, and applicable sales tax!


Inputs
Purchase Total The amount spent on the purchase before sales tax and interest
Sales Tax % The amount of sales tax to be charged (0-100%)
Credit Card Interest Rate (APR) This is the interest rate for the card or loan used for the purchase (0-100%). Usually, this can be found online or on your statement.
Monthly Payment Amount The anticipated amount that you'll be able to pay towards the credit card or loan every month. It is advised to pay more than the monthly minimum whenever possible.

A high-interest savings account is a great way to make your money work for you. Use our savings growth calculator to compare interest rates, and to understand the importance of making regular monthly deposits.


Inputs
Initial Deposit The amount of money that you will deposit into your savings account upon opening it.
Monthly Deposits The amount of money that you are planning to deposit into your savings account each month. In general, this should be between 10% and 20% of each paycheck.

Number of Months The number of months you would like to run the calculation for.

Loan Term (Months) This is the rate of return earned on your savings, taking into account the effect of compounding interest (0%-100%).

Inputs
Days between paychecks The number of days in between the paychecks you receive. For example, if you are paid every other Friday, this value is 14 days.

Percent to set aside for income tax (if required) If your employer does not withhold funds for year-end income tax payments, you may specify a percentage that you'd like to put aside here.
Percent to set aside for savings The percentage of erach paycheck to be set aside for savings. Generally, 20% is recommended, but this varies by situation.
